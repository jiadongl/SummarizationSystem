Australian researchers announced a  breakthrough Thursday in the search for the cause of schizophrenia,  linking the impaired thought processes involved with the disorder to  thinning grey matter in the brain.
Vaughan Carr of the Neuroscience Institute of Schizophrenia and  Allied Disorders said the discovery could provide a better  understanding of the genetic causes of the illness and eventually  lead to improved treatments.
Children of older fathers face an enhanced risk of developing schizophrenia when they grow older, according to research published on Friday.
